## Company Overview  
This analysis will focus on Tesla, Inc., a company in the automotive and clean energy industry that designs, manufactures, and sells electric vehicles, energy storage systems, and related technologies.

## Why Ratio Analysis Matters  
While financial statements provide essential information, they do not always reveal deeper insights into a company’s performance. Ratio analysis helps transform raw financial data into meaningful indicators that allow for better evaluation of financial health. Specifically, ratios highlight trends in profitability, operational efficiency, liquidity, and financial risk. They also allow for comparisons across time and against competitors. This makes it easier to identify strengths, weaknesses, and potential areas of concern that may not be immediately visible from standalone financial statements.

## Six Ratio Categories  
The analysis will focus on six key categories:  
- **Performance Ratios:** Measure overall returns generated for investors.  
- **Profitability Ratios:** Evaluate the company’s ability to generate earnings relative to revenue and assets.  
- **Efficiency Ratios:** Assess how effectively the company uses its assets and manages operations.  
- **Leverage Ratios:** Examine the extent of debt financing and financial risk.  
- **Liquidity Ratios:** Determine the company’s ability to meet short-term obligations.  
- **DuPont Analysis:** Breaks down return on equity into key drivers (profit margin, asset turnover, and leverage) for deeper insight.  

## Data Sources  
Financial data will be obtained from reliable and publicly available sources, including the company’s Form 10-K filings from the SEC EDGAR database, Tesla’s investor relations website, and financial platforms such as Yahoo Finance.
